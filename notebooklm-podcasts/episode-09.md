## Lesson 73: Hard Cap Purpose

Objective: Understand rigid bases.

Core concept: A hard cap carries shapes that hair alone cannot hold. It must fit, breathe, and avoid sharp edges.

Practice prompt: Name one hairstyle that might justify a hard cap.

Safety note: A real hard cap needs smooth edges, airflow, safe visibility, and no pressure points; discomfort or overheating is a stop signal.


Check question: What comfort issue would you check first?

Ready when: You can explain why a shell might exist.

Key terms:
- hard cap: A rigid or semi-rigid wig base built to hold sculptural shapes.
- shell: A rigid or semi-rigid base structure that carries a shape, usually hidden under hair.

## Lesson 75: Armature Choice

Objective: Pick a hidden skeleton only when needed.

Core concept: An armature can hold shape, but it also adds complexity, weight, and safety concerns.

Practice prompt: Choose whether a floating ahoge needs no support, wire, or foam.

Safety note: Real armatures need covered ends, smooth load paths, stable anchors, and no sharp, conductive, or concentrated pressure points.


Check question: What carries the load?

Ready when: You can choose or reject armature support.

Key terms:
- armature: A hidden support skeleton, often wire, foam, mesh, or plastic, that holds a shape.
- load path: The route weight and force take through the wig into anchors and the wearer's head.

## Lesson 76: Foam Core Coverage

Objective: Hide lightweight bulk.

Core concept: Foam can make large shapes lighter than solid hair, but it must be covered convincingly.

Practice prompt: Name how you would cover one foam shape: wefts, fiber sheet, or surface hair.

Safety note: Real foam work may create dust and use blades or adhesives; method-specific ventilation, dust protection, and guarded cutting are required.


Check question: Where might foam show?

Ready when: You can pair foam with a coverage method.

Key terms:
- foam core: A lightweight foam support used inside oversized spikes, buns, curls, or shapes.
- coverage: How well hair, fiber sheets, lace, or panels hide supports, gaps, and construction.

## Lesson 77: Wire Safety

Objective: Use wire carefully.

Core concept: Wire can support shapes, but ends and pressure points must be protected from the wearer and the wig fiber.

Practice prompt: Write one safety rule for wire inside a wig.

Safety note: Real wire supports need deburred, capped ends, strain relief, and enough separation from skin to prevent pokes or pressure injury.


Check question: Where could wire poke or bend badly?

Ready when: You can name one wire safety risk.

Key terms:
- wire support: Wire used to hold shape, spread load, or connect a piece to anchors.
- pressure point: A spot where weight, edge, clip, strap, or shell presses uncomfortably.

## Lesson 80: Ventilation Gap

Objective: Do not trap heat blindly.

Core concept: Rigid or dense builds need some comfort planning. Airflow and heat buildup matter before final finish.

Practice prompt: Name one place airflow could exist without ruining the look.

Safety note: A real rigid or dense build should be rejected if it traps unsafe heat, restricts breathing or vision, or cannot be removed quickly.

Key terms:
